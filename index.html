What is body mass index?

Body mass index or BMI is a measure to see weather someone has the right weight for their height. It is calculated by dividing your body mass by your height squared.
BMI can, but is not intended for finding out whether a person is fat or thin, as BMI does not say how many fat does someone have. BMI mainly says how much muscle or bone is in one's body. This may cause athletes to have higher BMI while still being very healthy. Even so, BMI is still very popular in giving information on weather somebody is healthy or not.

Category BMI (kg/m2)
Underweight (Severe thinness) < 16.0
Underweight (Moderate thinness) 16.0 – 16.9

Normal range 18.5 – 24.9
Overweight (Pre-obese) 25.0 – 29.9
Obese (Class I) 30.0 – 34.9
Obese (Class II) 35.0 – 39.9
Obese (Class III) ≥ 40.0
